Seasonal Affective Disorder (SAD) is a condition in which people do not get enough light in the winter time to entrain their circadian rhythms. This lack of entrainment leads to depression. Recently there has been hype around using SAD lights to target melanopsin, a non-vision photopigment found in intrinsically photosensitive retinal ganglion cells (iPRGC’s). However, lights that target melanopsin must be very high intensity and exposure times must be long. Since many of the commercially available SAD lights do not meet this intensity requirement and people do not look at them for long enough, the lights are ineffective at treating SAD. Circadian rhythm labs at the UW are inventing a new light based on recent scientific discoveries that does not require as high intensity of light and can be used for as little as one hour every morning. We are looking for undergraduates, technicians, grad students, postdocs, and faculty in circadian biology labs at the UW to help us test this new invention.
